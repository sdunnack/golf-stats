Score per Round
Total strokes per round over time. The dotted trend line shows your trajectory — a downward slope means improving.
Total Putts per Round
Total putts taken per round over time. Putts are one of the strongest predictors of score, so a downward trend here matters.
Greens in Regulation %
Percentage of holes where the green was reached in at most par − 2 shots. Higher is better; tour average is ~65%. More GIRs means more birdie looks.
Fairways Hit %
Percentage of par-4 and par-5 tee shots that landed in the fairway. Only holes where a fairway is trackable are counted.
Score vs Putts
Each dot is one round plotted by total putts (x-axis) vs total score (y-axis). A positive slope means more putts correlates with higher scores — the stronger this relationship, the more putting is driving your scoring.
Scoring Summary
Distribution of all hole outcomes — eagles, birdies, pars, bogeys, doubles, and worse — aggregated across every round in the current filter.
Par 3 / 4 / 5 Breakdown
Average score relative to par for each hole type. Zero means playing at par on average; positive values show where you’re losing the most strokes compared to the card.
Scoring by Round
Stacked bars showing the count of each outcome type (birdie, par, bogey, etc.) per round. Taller green sections mean more pars and birdies that round.
Scoring Distribution
Histogram of your 18-hole total scores across all filtered rounds. Each bar shows how many rounds fell in that score range. A tighter, left-shifted cluster indicates more consistent scoring.
Putting Summary
Breakdown of 1-putt, 2-putt, and 3-putt+ holes across all rounds. More 1-putts and fewer 3-putts directly lowers your score.
1-Putt % by Round
Percentage of holes converted with a single putt per round over time. Tour average is around 40%. An upward trend here indicates improving green reading or lag putting.
3-Putt Free Streak
Consecutive holes without a 3-putt, tracked continuously across rounds. Each bar is one streak; longer streaks mean sustained putting reliability. A new streak starts after every 3-putt.
Avg Putts by Hole
Average number of putts on each hole across all rounds. Holes with consistently high averages may indicate tricky greens or longer approach distances leaving harder lags.
Avg Score vs Par by Hole
Average strokes over or under par for each of the 18 holes. Bars above 0 mean you average over par on that hole. Quickly identifies your most and least scoreable holes.
GIR % by Hole
How often the green is reached in regulation on each hole. Holes with low GIR rates reveal where your approach game breaks down, whether from distance, lie, or club selection.
Fairway Miss Direction
Direction of missed fairways (left or right) per hole across all rounds. Reveals whether you have a consistent miss tendency on specific holes that could inform aim or club selection.
Penalties by Hole
Average penalty strokes per round for each hole. Highlights the highest-risk holes on the course.
Distance Distribution by Club
Box plot of shot distances for each club. The box covers the middle 50% of shots; the center line is the median; whiskers extend to typical extremes. Wider boxes mean less consistent contact. Requires CT10 sensor data.
Shot Distance by Lie
Average shot distance grouped by starting lie (tee box, fairway, rough, etc.). Shows how much lie conditions affect your carry distance. Requires CT10 sensor data.
Club Usage Frequency
Number of tracked shots hit with each club across all rounds. Requires CT10 sensor data.
Driving Distance Trend
Distance of tee shots on par-4 and par-5 holes over time, filtered to shots starting on the tee box. The trend line shows whether your driving distance is increasing or decreasing. Requires CT10 sensor data.
